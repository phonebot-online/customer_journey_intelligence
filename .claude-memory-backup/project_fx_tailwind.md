Phonebot imports refurb phones from UK suppliers (priced in GBP) and sells in AUD. Currency moves are a structural input to gross margin that has nothing to do with operations or marketing.

**As of 2026-04-30:** AUD/GBP is at 0.5289, up **+11.05% YoY** (1Y window per xe.com). That means GBP-denominated COGS is ~11% cheaper in AUD terms vs April 2025.

**Estimated impact at mid (65%) GBP-share of COGS:** ~A$25k of additional GP per month vs prior-year FX. That's about 16% of April 2026 net GP coming from currency, not the underlying business.

**How to apply:**
- For any YoY comparison of GP, GP%, or profit-after-spend, **report both headline and FX-stripped values** so the user can see what's operations vs FX.
- Bracket the FX-stripped number using GBP-share assumptions of 50% / 65% / 80% of total COGS (the share isn't precisely measured).
- MoM comparisons (e.g. March vs April 2026) are NOT affected by FX since the rate is roughly stable over a few weeks.
- If AUD weakens vs GBP, the same model should be used in reverse — the tailwind becomes a headwind.
