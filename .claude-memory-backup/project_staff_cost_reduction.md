In the year leading up to April 2026, Phonebot reduced AU dispatch operations: cut 2 senior managerial/dispatch staff because order volume was lower (less dispatch team needed → less managerial cost above them). **AU staff wages alone are down ~A$14,000/month vs April 2025.**

Additionally, Pakistan-based staff costs are also down by ~A$8,500/month YoY. **However, those Pakistan staff worked on multiple projects, not exclusively Phonebot — attribution to Phonebot specifically is partial.** Use a 50% attribution as default (≈ $4,250/mo Phonebot share) when including in YoY operating-profit math, but show sensitivity 0%/50%/100% so the user can pick.

Combined attributable staff savings (mid attribution): ~$18,250/mo YoY. High attribution: ~$22,500/mo. Low attribution (AU-only): $14,000/mo.

**How to apply:**
- Staff costs sit BELOW the gross-profit line (OpEx, not COGS). They do NOT appear in the Total / Cost Price / GP columns of order CSVs.
- For YoY operating-profit comparisons: ADD ~A$14k/month savings to the Apr 2026 side that doesn't show up in GP.
- This is a structural ongoing reduction, not one-off — applies forward indefinitely until staffing changes again.
- The savings PARTIALLY offset the underlying business decline (FX-stripped GP is down ~$25-40k YoY; staff savings recover ~$14k of that). Net of FX + staff, the business is roughly flat YoY at operating-profit level.
- Treat the FX tailwind, staff savings, and supply-shock impact as three separate accounting layers when building any "am I better off" comparison.
