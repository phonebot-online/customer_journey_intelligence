## Scale plan from here — ranked by realistic monthly upside

### 1. FB incrementality holdout test (HIGHEST LEVERAGE — A$11k/mo upside if non-incremental)

This is your single biggest available move. Current FB spend A$243/day = ~A$7,300/30d. PM-attributed GP is roughly A$31/day. Net: -A$212/day = -A$6,360/30d = -A$76k/year. The 12-month locked number is -A$133k/year because spend was higher historically.

**Protocol:**
- **Week 1-2 of test:** Pause the lowest-attributed FB campaign (likely cold prospecting "Cold | TOF | 2 ad sets...") at 50% budget. Keep retargeting + brand campaigns alive at full budget so you don't kill any actual incremental signal.
- **Watchpoints (daily):**
  - CMS daily orders — the dashboard chart. Should track at 22-27/day baseline.
  - GA4 sessions from `facebook / cpc` — currently near zero, but watch for further drop or no change.
  - Branded "phonebot" search clicks in GSC — was 32-44% down concurrent with prior FB cuts. Will it drop further during the holdout?
- **Decision criteria after 14 days:**
  - If CMS daily orders unchanged (within ±5% of trailing 30d): FB cold is non-incremental. Cut it permanently. **+A$3-4k/mo locked**.
  - If CMS orders drop 5-15%: FB has some incremental contribution. Restore at half-volume permanently. **+A$1-2k/mo**.
  - If CMS orders drop >15%: FB cold is incremental. Keep it. (My priors say this is unlikely but the test is the only way to know.)
- **Cost of running the test:** A$3-4k of foregone FB spend over 2 weeks = ~A$0 risk because the platform-attributed GP from those campaigns is so small.

This is the experiment with the largest expected information value relative to its cost in the entire plan.

### 2. Cut the marginal AW campaigns (A$1-2k/mo upside, near-zero risk)

The four campaigns at real ROAS 1.1-1.2× (CJ PMax (Apple), SS All Products >$1000, PMax Apple Watches, plus you can include CJ Refurbished Phones at 1.33× as a maybe) are spending A$4,124/30d for A$556 net profit. ### 4. PMax (Apple) iPad — the iPad recovery question

This is your biggest current campaign (A$121/day, 1.72× real ROAS) and the one you might want to scale to attack the A$53k iPad gap. **Be honest about the math:**

- At current 1.72× real ROAS, A$100 of incremental spend yields A$172 GP, A$72 net.
- To recover A$53k/mo of iPad GP gap, need A$31k incremental spend = A$1,034/day extra. That's far past the saturating point.
- A more realistic 50% bump (A$121/day → A$180/day = +A$59/day spend) at likely degraded marginal ROAS of ~1.3× would yield +A$77 GP/day, net +A$18/day = **+A$540/mo**. Modest.
- A 100% bump (A$240/day) at marginal ROAS ~1.0× would yield ~+A$60/day net = **+A$1,800/mo**. Also modest.

**Conclusion:** PMax (Apple) iPad has limited paid-media headroom. The iPad gap is mostly inventory/organic/competitive, not a paid-budget problem. Bump it 50% as a controlled test, watch ROAS for 2 weeks, and don't push past 1.0× marginal.
